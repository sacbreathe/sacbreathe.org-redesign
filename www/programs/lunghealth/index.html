About

Our lung health programs are made possible by the Breathe Lung Health Collaborative. The Lung Health Collaborative is a partnership between health professionals and community members looking to improve outcomes for people with asthma in our region. The Collaborative meets quarterly to discuss new resources, update informational materials, and coordinate outreach and education activities in the community.

Parent Asthma Classes

In partnership with with SETA's Headstart Program and UC Davis Health System, BREATHE provides free asthma education classes to low-income families living in communities with high rates of childhood asthma.

Lead by Dr. Arif Seyal, Chief of Allergy, Asthma and Clinical Immunology at North Valley Kaiser Permanente Medical Center, these classes teach families how to proactively manage their children's asthma symptoms by identifying and reducing environmental triggers, strictly adhering to their prescribed treatment plans, and maintaining a healthy, active lifestyle.

Now entering it's third year, our parent asthma classes have reached more than 150 households through classes held at five SETA Headstart Centers. This year, with generous funding through UC Davis Health System and Kaiser Permanente, we will be continuing to make a direct impact on our community's health with the development and expansion of this valuable education program.
